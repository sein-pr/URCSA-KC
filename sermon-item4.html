Full description

In John 5:1-15, the passage describes the healing of a man who had been paralyzed for 38 years. Jesus visits the Pool of Bethesda, where many sick people gathered, hoping for healing when the waters were stirred. He notices the man and asks if he wants to be healed. The man explains his struggle to reach the pool, as others always get there before him.... Read more

As John recounts the story, he notices Jesus arriving at the pool. Jesus spots a man who has been paralyzed for 38 long years, an incredibly poignant detail that John emphasizes to illustrate the man’s profound suffering. John watches as Jesus approaches the man and poses a seemingly simple question: “Do you want to be healed?” The man responds by sharing his struggle to reach the water, revealing a deep sense of hopelessness and isolation. John captures the man’s vulnerability in this moment, highlighting the despair that has defined his life. Then, John witnesses the moment of transformation. Jesus commands the man, “Get up! Pick up your mat and walk.” Immediately, the man obeys, rising to his feet and walking away. John marvels at the power of Jesus’ words, recognizing this miracle as a profound demonstration of divine authority.

However, John also sees the tensions that arise from this healing. The miracle takes place on the Sabbath, prompting the Jewish religious leaders to confront the man as he carries his mat. They question him about breaking their laws, illustrating the rigid interpretation of the Sabbath that John has seen time and again. The man explains that it was Jesus who healed him, which only adds to the friction between Jesus and the religious authorities. Later, John observes Jesus finding the man in the temple. Jesus advises him to “stop sinning, or something worse may happen.” Here, John perceives a deeper message—Jesus not only brings physical healing but also emphasizes the importance of spiritual well-being.

Thinks

  • T A Place of Need: Jesus choose to visit the pool near the Sheep Gate in Jerusalem on purpose. What does it tell us about His care for those in desperate situations?
  • Enduring Suffering: The man by the pool had been sick for 38 years. How often do we find ourselves waiting for a change, yearning for healing, but facing seemingly insurmountable obstacles?
  • Surprising Question: Jesus asks the man, "Do you want to get well?" This is an obvious question, but Jesus asks it to prepare the man for receiving God’s grace. What does this reveal about the role of our own willingness in receiving healing?
  • Challenging Traditions: The healing happened on the Sabbath, leading to controversy. How does this incident challenge our views on rules and religious traditions and what does it reveal about Jesus' perspective on compassion and divine intervention?
  • Discovering Identity: The healed man didn't initially know who Jesus was. How often do we encounter Christ in unexpected places, and what impact does it have on our understanding of faith?
  • A Serious Message: Jesus warned the man about sin, suggesting that spiritual healing is also important. What does this mean for us, and how does it connect with the theme of healing in our lives?

Prayers

  1. Thank God for people in your life.

    Heavenly Father,
    Thank You for the wonderful people You have placed in my life. I am grateful for their support, love, and encouragement. Please bless them and guide us all in our journey together. Help us to cherish and uplift one another. Amen

  2. Prayer for Strength in Adversity

    Lord,
    In times of struggle and confusion, remind me that I am not alone. Just as You were with Jeremiah, be my strength and comfort. Help me to trust in Your power and to find hope in Your promises. Amen.

  3. Prayer for people who are lonely.

    Gracious God,
    WI lift up to You all those who are feeling lonely and isolated. Please surround them with Your love and comfort. Help them to feel Your presence and remind them that they are never truly alone. I ask that You bring people into their lives who can offer companionship and support. May they find joy and hope in the connections that are formed, and may Your peace fill their hearts. Amen.
